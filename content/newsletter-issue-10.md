### Question 2: "My friend says AI will take my son's job. Should I be worried?" â€” Graham, 71

**Honest answer:** Probably not, but change is coming.

AI is very good at:
- Repetitive tasks
- Data processing
- Basic writing and coding
- Pattern recognition

AI is NOT good at:
- Building relationships
- Physical work in unpredictable environments
- Creative problem-solving
- Empathy and emotional intelligence

**The pattern throughout history:** Technology changes jobs more than it eliminates them. Calculators didn't eliminate accountants. ATMs didn't eliminate bank tellers. Email didn't eliminate postal workers (well, maybe a bit).

**Best advice for your son:** Learn to work WITH AI tools, not compete against them. The people who'll struggle are those who refuse to adapt.

### Question 3: "I asked ChatGPT a question and the answer was wrong. Can I trust anything it says?" â€” Margaret, 73

**This is completely normal.** ChatGPT makes mistakes. It's called "hallucination" â€” the AI generates confident-sounding text that's factually incorrect.

**Why it happens:** AI predicts the most likely next words based on patterns. Sometimes those patterns lead to plausible-sounding nonsense.

**How often:** For general knowledge, ChatGPT is correct about 85-95% of the time. For specific facts, dates, and recent events, accuracy drops.

**Your strategy:**
1. Use AI for drafts, ideas, and understanding â€” not as a fact book
2. Always verify important information
3. Ask it: "Are you confident about this? What are your sources?"
4. Cross-check with Google for critical facts

**Think of AI like a brilliant friend who occasionally gets their facts mixed up.** Listen to them, but verify the important stuff.

### Question 4: "Is it worth paying for ChatGPT Plus ($20/month)?" â€” Robert, 68

**For most beginners: No. The free version is excellent.**

**Consider paying if:**
- You use ChatGPT daily for important tasks
- You need faster response times (free version can be slow during peak hours)
- You want the latest AI model (GPT-4 is noticeably better at complex tasks)
- You want to upload images/documents for AI to analyse

**Stay free if:**
- You use it a few times a week
- Your tasks are straightforward (emails, questions, planning)
- You're still learning and experimenting

**My advice:** Use the free version for 2-3 months. If you find yourself wanting more, then upgrade. Most people never need to pay.
