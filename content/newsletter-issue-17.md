### Australian Schools Embrace AI ‚Äî With Guardrails

The Australian Curriculum is officially updating to include "AI literacy" as a core competency from Year 5 onwards.

**What students will learn:**
- How AI works (conceptually, not technically)
- When to use AI and when not to
- How to verify AI-generated information
- Privacy and data awareness
- Ethical considerations

**Why this matters to you:**
Your grandchildren will soon be learning about AI in school. The more you understand, the more you can discuss it with them ‚Äî and perhaps learn together.

## üõ†Ô∏è Tutorial: Conversations About AI

### For Primary School Age (5-12)

**Start with what they already know:**
"You know how you can ask Siri questions? That's a type of AI. It's a computer that's learned to understand and answer questions ‚Äî kind of like how you learn things at school."

**Make it relatable:**
"When you play video games, the characters that aren't controlled by you ‚Äî that's AI too. The game learned how to make them move and react."

**Introduce limits early:**
"AI is really smart at some things, but it doesn't actually understand like you do. Sometimes it makes mistakes. It's a tool, not a friend who always knows best."

### For High School Age (13-17)

**Acknowledge what they already know:**
They likely know more about certain AI tools than you do. Start by asking what they use and how.

**Focus on critical thinking:**
"How do you know if something AI created is true? What would you check?"

**Discuss the ethics:**
"Is it fair to use AI for homework? Why or why not? Where do you draw the line?"

**Talk about careers:**
"AI is changing jobs. Not necessarily replacing them ‚Äî but changing what skills matter. What skills do you think will be most valuable?"

### The Question to Ask ANY Age

"Can you show me how you use it?"

Let them teach you. You'll learn what they actually know (and don't know), and they'll feel respected rather than lectured.

### 1. Khan Academy's Khanmigo
- **What it is:** AI tutor designed for students
- **Age:** Primary and high school
- **Safety:** Guardrails built in, designed for education

### 2. Duolingo
- **What it is:** Language learning with AI
- **Age:** 5+
- **Safety:** Very safe, designed for all ages
- **Cost:** Free with premium option
- **Why we like it:** Excellent for learning together

### 3. ChatGPT (with supervision)
- **What it is:** General AI assistant
- **Age:** 13+ (according to OpenAI)
- **Safety:** Requires parent involvement
- **Cost:** Free
- **Why we like it:** Real-world AI literacy

## üí° Activities to Do Together

### Activity 1: "AI or Human?"
Show them writing samples ‚Äî some from AI, some from humans. Can they tell the difference? Discuss what gives it away.

### Activity 2: "Check the Facts"
Ask AI a question together. Then verify the answer using other sources. Did AI get it right?

### Activity 3: "AI Art Gallery"
Use a free AI image generator together. Talk about what's impressive, what's weird, and what it means for artists.

### Activity 4: "The Homework Debate"
Discuss: "If AI can write your essay, what's the point of learning to write?" (There's no single right answer ‚Äî the conversation is the point.)

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Important Safety Notes

### For Younger Children
- Don't let them use AI chat tools alone
- Monitor what they're asking and sharing
- Never let them share personal information
- Keep it supervised and educational

### For Teenagers
- Have conversations, not just rules
- Discuss why schools have AI policies
- Talk about the difference between "using AI" and "letting AI do everything"
- Model good AI use yourself

### For All Ages
- AI tools collect data ‚Äî discuss what that means
- Critical thinking is the skill that matters most
- The goal isn't to fear AI ‚Äî it's to use it wisely

## üìß Reader Question

*"My 14-year-old grandson uses ChatGPT for everything. His parents don't seem worried, but I am. Should I be?"* ‚Äî Robert, 70

Robert, your concern shows you care. Here's how I'd think about it:

**It depends on HOW he's using it:**
- Using AI to understand concepts? Great.
- Using AI to draft and then improve his own writing? Fine.
- Copying AI output as his own work without thinking? That's a problem.

**Questions to ask (not lecture ‚Äî ask):**
- "Show me how you use it for school"
- "Does your teacher know you use AI?"
- "What's the difference between using AI to help you learn and using AI to skip learning?"

**The bigger picture:**
AI literacy is a skill his generation needs. Complete avoidance isn't realistic. Teaching wise use is the goal.

Your role? Stay curious, stay involved, stay in conversation.
