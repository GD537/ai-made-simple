### Scam Alert: AI-Powered Investment Fraud Is Surging

Australian authorities have warned about a dramatic increase in AI-powered investment scams. Here's what to watch for:

**The scam:** Fake investment platforms using AI-generated videos of celebrities (Elon Musk, Mark Zuckerberg, even Australian politicians) endorsing "guaranteed return" schemes. The videos look incredibly real.

**How to spot it:**
- No legitimate investment guarantees returns
- Celebrity endorsements for investment apps are almost always fake
- If someone contacts you unsolicited about an investment, it's a scam
- Real investment platforms are registered with ASIC (check asic.gov.au)

**Rule of thumb:** If it sounds too good to be true, it is. AI just makes the lie more convincing.

### Budgeting Help

> "I'm retired on a pension of $2,100/fortnight. My fixed expenses are: rent $600/fortnight, car insurance $150/month, health insurance $200/month, medications $80/month, phone $45/month. Help me create a simple budget for food, entertainment, and savings."

### Comparing Options

> "I'm comparing two savings accounts. Account A offers 4.5% with no fees but requires $2,000 minimum. Account B offers 5.1% but charges $10/month. I'll keep about $15,000 in the account. Which is better over 12 months? Show me the maths."

AI does the calculation and shows its working.

### Tax Time Preparation

> "I'm a retired Australian on the Age Pension. I also have a part-time job earning $12,000/year and receive dividends from shares worth about $3,000/year. What tax deductions should I look into? What records should I keep?"

**Remember:** This is general education. Your specific tax situation needs a tax agent or accountant.
