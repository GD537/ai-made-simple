### AI and Privacy: What You Need to Know

There's been a lot of talk about AI and privacy this week, after a major report revealed how much data AI companies collect. Here's the plain truth:

**What AI companies typically collect:**
- The questions you ask (to improve the AI)
- Your email address (for your account)
- General usage patterns (how often you use it)

**What they DON'T collect (usually):**
- Your photos (unless you upload them)
- Your location (unless you share it)
- Your personal files (unless you paste them in)

**How to protect yourself:**
1. Don't share sensitive information (bank details, passwords, Medicare numbers) with AI
2. Check privacy settings in ChatGPT: Settings ‚Üí Data Controls ‚Üí Turn off "Improve the model for everyone"
3. Use AI in private/incognito mode for sensitive questions
4. Read privacy policies (or better yet ‚Äî ask ChatGPT to summarise them for you!)

**The bottom line:** AI is generally safe to use for everyday tasks. Just treat it like a public conversation ‚Äî don't say anything you wouldn't want a stranger to hear.

### iPhone (iOS 18+)

**1. Siri + AI**
Siri has gotten dramatically smarter. Try:
- "Hey Siri, summarise my unread emails"
- "Hey Siri, create a reminder based on this text message"
- "Hey Siri, what did John say in his last message?"

**2. Photos App**
- Open Photos ‚Üí Search ‚Üí Type anything: "beach sunset," "birthday cake," "dog"
- Your phone finds matching photos using AI ‚Äî even if you never tagged them
- Try: "Photos of documents" (finds all your scanned docs!)

**3. Live Text**
- Point your camera at text (a menu, sign, book page)

**4. Writing Tools (iOS 18.1+)**
- Select text in any app ‚Üí tap "Writing Tools"

### Android Phones

**1. Google Assistant + AI**

**Use voice typing for AI chatbots.**
