How to Use AI as Your Personal Travel Agent

By Sam Digital · February 2026

Remember when planning a trip meant calling travel agents, flipping through guidebooks, and hoping you didn't miss something important? Those days are gone. Today, you can plan an entire vacation from your living room—and it won't cost you a penny.

AI tools like ChatGPT and Google's Gemini can act as your personal travel planner. They can suggest destinations, create day-by-day itineraries, recommend restaurants, help you pack, and even estimate your budget. Best of all, they're available 24/7 and won't judge you for asking the same question twice.

In this guide, I'll show you exactly how to use AI to plan your next trip, step by step. No tech expertise required—just curiosity and a sense of adventure.

What You'll Need

Before we dive in, here's what you need:

  • A free ChatGPT account (go to chat.openai.com and sign up with your email)
  • OR a free Google account (to use Gemini at gemini.google.com)
  • Basic information about your trip (where you want to go, how long you'll stay, what you enjoy)

Both ChatGPT and Gemini work similarly, so choose whichever you're more comfortable with. For this guide, I'll use ChatGPT examples, but the same approach works with Gemini.

Step 1: Start with the Big Picture

The key to getting great travel advice from AI is being specific about what you want. Don't just say "plan a trip to Italy." Give it context—who's traveling, what you enjoy, and any limitations you have.

Here's a good starting prompt:

You: "I'm planning a 7-day trip to Italy in September with my husband. We're in our 60s, enjoy history and good food, but prefer not to do too much walking. We'd like to visit Rome, Florence, and maybe one smaller town. Can you suggest an itinerary?"

See how much information that packs in? You've told ChatGPT:

  • How long you're going
  • When you're going
  • Who's going
  • Your interests
  • Your limitations
  • Cities you're interested in

Within seconds, ChatGPT will create a detailed day-by-day plan, with suggestions for how many days to spend in each city and what to see.

Pro tip: The more details you provide upfront, the better the suggestions. Mention your budget range, travel pace (relaxed vs. packed), dietary restrictions, mobility concerns, or whether you're traveling with kids or grandkids.

Step 2: Refine Your Itinerary

Once ChatGPT gives you the first draft, don't be shy about asking for changes. This is where AI really shines—it's infinitely patient with tweaks and adjustments.

Let's say the itinerary includes too much walking. Just say:

You: "This looks great, but Day 3 has a lot of walking. Can you adjust it to include more sit-down activities and maybe a lunch break at a nice cafe?"

Or maybe you want to swap cities:

You: "Can you replace Florence with Venice? And add a day trip from Rome to a small coastal town?"

ChatGPT remembers the conversation, so it will update the itinerary while keeping the rest of your preferences intact. You can keep refining until it feels just right.

Step 3: Get Restaurant Recommendations

One of the best parts of using AI for travel planning is getting local food recommendations. You can ask for specific types of restaurants, neighborhoods, or cuisines.

Try prompts like these:

You: "Recommend 5 authentic Italian restaurants in Rome that aren't too touristy, with outdoor seating if possible."
You: "I need a good lunch spot near the Vatican that's wheelchair accessible and serves pasta."
You: "What are some highly-rated casual restaurants in Florence where we can try local specialties without breaking the bank?"

ChatGPT will give you restaurant names, what they're known for, and sometimes even dish recommendations. Just keep in mind: always double-check reviews on Google Maps or TripAdvisor before you go, since AI can occasionally suggest outdated or closed restaurants.

Step 4: Create a Packing List

Packing is where AI becomes surprisingly helpful. Instead of worrying you've forgotten something, let ChatGPT create a customized packing list based on your trip.

Here's how to ask:

You: "Create a packing list for a 7-day trip to Italy in September. We'll be in cities, doing some light walking, dining out, and visiting churches. I'm a woman in my 60s."

ChatGPT will generate a thorough list, including:

  • Clothing suggestions (layers for September weather, comfortable walking shoes, a scarf for churches)
  • Travel essentials (passport, travel adapter, medications)
  • Toiletries and personal items
  • Tech items (phone charger, camera)
  • Extras (reusable water bottle, daypack)

You can ask follow-ups like:

You: "I'm trying to pack light—can you narrow this down to carry-on only?"

Or:

You: "What should I pack for occasional rain?"

Step 5: Plan Your Daily Schedule

A day-by-day itinerary is nice, but what about the details? AI can break down each day hour by hour, so you know exactly what to do and when.

You: "Give me a detailed schedule for Day 2 in Rome—visiting the Colosseum, Roman Forum, and having lunch nearby. Include travel times and breaks."

ChatGPT might respond with something like:

9:00 AM – Start at the Colosseum (book tickets in advance to skip lines)
11:00 AM – Walk to the Roman Forum (5-minute walk)
12:30 PM – Lunch at Taverna dei Fori Imperiali (casual, nearby, great carbonara)
2:00 PM – Rest break at your hotel or a cafe
4:00 PM – Evening stroll through Trastevere neighborhood
7:00 PM – Dinner at a local trattoria

This takes the guesswork out of your day. You'll know where to be, how long things take, and when to build in rest time.

Step 6: Budget Planning

Worried about costs? AI can help you estimate a realistic budget and suggest ways to save money.

You: "Estimate a daily budget for two people in Rome, including mid-range hotels, meals, attractions, and local transport."

ChatGPT will break down average costs:

  • Hotel: $120–180/night for a mid-range hotel
  • Meals: $60–80/day (breakfast, lunch, dinner for two)
  • Attractions: $40–60/day (museum tickets, tours)
  • Transport: $10–15/day (metro, buses, taxis)

You can also ask:

You: "How can we save money on food without sacrificing quality?"

Or:

You: "What are some free or low-cost activities in Florence?"

Step 7: Handling Special Situations

AI is great for problem-solving those little trip details that can feel overwhelming.

Getting Around

You: "What's the best way to get from Rome to Florence? Train or car rental?"

Language Help

You: "Teach me 10 essential Italian phrases for dining out and asking for directions."

Accessibility

You: "Which Rome attractions are wheelchair accessible, and how do I get there?"

Travel Insurance

You: "What should I look for in travel insurance for a 7-day European trip? Do I need it?"

Real-World Example: Full Planning Session

Let me show you what a complete planning session might look like, from start to finish:

You: "I want to plan a 5-day trip to Portugal in October. I'm traveling solo, enjoy wine, coastal views, and historic sites. Budget is moderate. Where should I go?"

ChatGPT: [Suggests Lisbon + Sintra + Porto, with a day-by-day breakdown]

You: "Can you add a day trip to a vineyard from Porto?"

ChatGPT: [Updates itinerary with Douro Valley wine tour]

You: "Create a packing list for October weather in Portugal."

ChatGPT: [Provides list with layers, rain jacket, comfortable shoes]

You: "Suggest 3 good seafood restaurants in Lisbon near the coast."

ChatGPT: [Lists restaurants with descriptions]

You: "Estimate total costs for 5 days—flights not included."

ChatGPT: [Breaks down accommodation, food, activities, transport]

In one conversation, you've planned an entire trip. No phone calls, no travel agent fees, no stress.

Important Limitations to Remember

AI is incredibly helpful, but it's not perfect. Here's what to watch out for:

Always Verify Details

ChatGPT might suggest a restaurant that's closed or an attraction that's under renovation. Always cross-check with Google Maps, official websites, or recent reviews.

Outdated Information

AI doesn't have real-time data. Prices, hours, and availability can change. Use AI for planning, then confirm the specifics closer to your trip.

Local Expertise Matters

AI can give you the highlights, but locals know the hidden gems. Once you're there, don't be afraid to ask your hotel, a tour guide, or friendly locals for recommendations.

Book Yourself

ChatGPT can't make reservations or book tickets. Use its suggestions, then book directly through official websites or trusted platforms.

Smart approach: Use AI as your research assistant and idea generator. Then verify the details and book through reliable sources. Think of it as a helpful starting point, not the final word.

Tips for Getting the Best Results

Here's how to make AI work harder for you:

  • Be specific. "Plan a trip to Europe" is too vague. "Plan a 10-day trip to Spain and Portugal for a couple in their 50s who love wine and history" is perfect.
  • Mention constraints. Budget limits, mobility issues, dietary needs—share these upfront so the suggestions fit your reality.
  • Ask follow-ups. Don't settle for the first answer. Ask "Can you make this cheaper?" or "What if we skip that city and go somewhere quieter?"
  • Request alternatives. "Give me 3 different restaurant options in this area" ensures you have backups.
  • Save the conversation. ChatGPT saves your chats. You can return later to review or continue planning.

Beyond the Basics: Creative Uses

Once you're comfortable with basic trip planning, try these:

Create a "First-Timer's Guide"

You: "I've never been to Paris. What are the top 10 things I absolutely shouldn't miss, and what can I skip?"

Plan Around Your Interests

You: "I'm a bird watcher visiting Costa Rica. Suggest a 5-day itinerary focused on bird watching, with beginner-friendly locations."

Solo Travel Advice

You: "I'm a woman in my 60s traveling alone to Ireland. What safety tips should I know, and which cities are best for solo travelers?"

Multi-City European Trip

You: "Plan a 3-week trip hitting London, Paris, Amsterdam, and Berlin. How should I split my time, and what's the best order to visit them?"

The Bottom Line

Planning a trip used to mean hours of research, phone calls, and second-guessing yourself. Now, you can have a personalized travel plan in minutes—and it's completely free.

AI won't replace the joy of discovery when you're actually traveling, but it can eliminate the stress and uncertainty of planning. Use it to create your roadmap, then let yourself wander and explore once you're there.

The best part? You can plan at your own pace, from the comfort of your couch, with a cup of tea in hand. No pressure, no judgment, just you and your curiosity about the world.

So pick a destination, open ChatGPT, and start planning. Your next adventure is just a conversation away.
