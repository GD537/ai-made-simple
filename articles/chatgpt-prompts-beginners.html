25 ChatGPT Prompts Everyone Should Know

By Sam Digital · February 2026

You've created a ChatGPT account. You've typed a few questions. But now you're staring at that blank text box wondering: What should I actually ask?

Here's the truth: ChatGPT is only as useful as the prompts you give it. A vague question gets a vague answer. But a well-crafted prompt? That's where the magic happens.

Think of it like this: If you walked up to a helpful stranger and said "help me," they'd be confused. But if you said "I need directions to the nearest pharmacy," they could actually assist you. ChatGPT is the same—the clearer and more specific you are, the better the response.

Below are 25 prompts that work beautifully for everyday tasks. I've organized them by category so you can find what you need quickly. Each one includes an explanation of why it works and a tip for customizing it to your situation.

How to use these prompts: Copy the prompt, replace the parts in [brackets] with your own details, paste it into ChatGPT, and hit Enter. That's it. You can adjust the wording to sound more like you—ChatGPT doesn't need formal language.

Writing & Communication

Whether you're drafting an email, writing a card, or just need help putting your thoughts into words, these prompts make writing easier.

1"Write a polite but firm email to [company] about [issue]. I've been a customer for [X years]."

This prompt works because it gives ChatGPT three key pieces of information: the recipient, the problem, and your relationship with them. That context helps it strike the right tone—respectful but assertive. The result is usually a well-structured email that gets your point across without sounding rude or overly harsh.

Pro tip: Add details like "I've already tried calling twice" or "The issue started on January 15th" to make the email more specific and credible.
2"Help me write a thank-you note to [person] for [what they did]."

Thank-you notes can feel awkward to write, especially if you're not sure how formal or casual to be. This prompt handles that for you. By specifying the person and what they did, ChatGPT can craft a message that feels personal and genuine—not generic or stiff.

Pro tip: Mention how you know the person ("my neighbor," "my son's teacher") so ChatGPT can adjust the tone appropriately.
3"Rewrite this to sound more professional: [paste text]"

Maybe you've written something that sounds too casual or rambling, and you need to clean it up. This prompt is perfect for that. ChatGPT will take your rough draft and polish it—fixing grammar, removing slang, and tightening the language—while keeping your core message intact.

Pro tip: You can also ask for "more friendly," "more formal," or "shorter" versions depending on the situation.
4"Proofread this and fix any mistakes: [paste text]"

ChatGPT is excellent at catching typos, grammar errors, and awkward phrasing. This prompt turns it into a free proofreader. It's especially useful for important emails, letters, or anything you're sending to someone outside your immediate circle.

Pro tip: Ask it to "explain what you fixed" if you want to learn from your mistakes and improve your writing over time.
5"Write a birthday/anniversary/sympathy message for [person]. They are [details]."

Finding the right words for emotionally significant moments can be tough. This prompt helps you create heartfelt messages without sounding clichéd or insincere. The more details you provide—age, personality, relationship—the more personalized the message will be.

Pro tip: Include a specific memory or detail ("They love gardening" or "We've known each other since high school") to make the message feel more authentic.

Health & Wellness

ChatGPT isn't a doctor, but it can help you prepare for appointments, understand medical terms, and think about health-related questions.

6"I'm seeing my doctor about [issue]. I'm [age]. What questions should I ask?"

Doctor's appointments can be overwhelming, and it's easy to forget what you wanted to ask. This prompt helps you prepare a list of relevant questions so you make the most of your appointment. It's especially helpful if you're nervous or dealing with something new.

Pro tip: Mention any symptoms or concerns you have ("I've been feeling dizzy," "It's been going on for two weeks") for more tailored questions.
7"Explain what [medical term] means in simple English."

Medical jargon can be confusing and intimidating. This prompt translates complicated terms into plain language so you can understand what your doctor, pharmacist, or test results are actually saying. Knowledge is empowering, especially when it comes to your health.

Pro tip: If the explanation is still confusing, follow up with "explain it even simpler" or "give me an example."
8"Create a gentle exercise plan for someone who is [age] with [conditions]."

Starting an exercise routine can feel daunting, especially if you have mobility issues, chronic conditions, or haven't been active in a while. This prompt generates a realistic, low-impact plan that takes your limitations into account. Always run any exercise plan by your doctor first, but this gives you a helpful starting point.

Pro tip: Specify your fitness level ("complete beginner," "moderately active") and any equipment you have ("no equipment," "I have a stationary bike").
9"I have [dietary need]. Create 5 easy dinner recipes."

Whether you're managing diabetes, avoiding gluten, or following a low-sodium diet, finding suitable recipes can be time-consuming. This prompt generates meal ideas that fit your dietary restrictions without requiring exotic ingredients or complicated techniques.

Pro tip: Add preferences like "quick to make," "uses simple ingredients," or "no more than 5 steps" to get recipes that match your cooking style.
10"What questions should I ask my pharmacist about [medication]?"

Pharmacists are an underused resource. This prompt helps you think through important questions about side effects, interactions, timing, and storage so you can use your medication safely and effectively.

Pro tip: Mention other medications or supplements you take ("I also take a multivitamin and blood pressure medication") for more relevant questions.

Travel & Planning

Planning a trip—whether it's a weekend getaway or an international adventure—becomes much easier with these prompts.

11"Plan a [X]-day trip to [destination] for [who]. Budget [amount]. We enjoy [interests]."

This is one of the most powerful prompts for travel planning. By providing the length, destination, travelers, budget, and interests, you get a customized itinerary that actually fits your situation—not a generic tourist guide. It's like having a travel agent in your pocket.

Pro tip: Mention mobility needs ("We prefer not to walk long distances"), dietary restrictions, or pace preferences ("We like a relaxed schedule") for even better suggestions.
12"I'm in [city]. Recommend 3 restaurants for dinner tonight. Budget [amount]."

When you're traveling or just don't feel like cooking, this prompt saves you from endless scrolling through review sites. It gives you quick, budget-appropriate options so you can make a decision and move on with your evening.

Pro tip: Specify cuisine type ("Italian," "seafood"), dietary needs, or atmosphere ("quiet and romantic," "family-friendly") for more targeted recommendations.
13"Create a packing list for [trip details]."

Forgetting something important can ruin a trip. This prompt generates a comprehensive packing list based on your destination, duration, and planned activities. It's especially useful for trips to unfamiliar climates or activities you don't do often.

Pro tip: Include details like "beach resort in Mexico, 5 days, winter" or "hiking trip in Scotland, 1 week, summer" for the most accurate list.
14"What should I know about visiting [destination]? Local customs, tipping, safety?"

Every destination has its own etiquette and unwritten rules. This prompt helps you avoid embarrassing cultural missteps and prepares you for what to expect. It's particularly useful for international travel where norms can be very different from home.

Pro tip: Ask follow-up questions like "How much should I tip at restaurants?" or "Is tap water safe to drink?" for specific concerns.
15"Compare [destination A] vs [destination B] for a [description] holiday."

Choosing between two destinations can be agonizing. This prompt lays out the pros and cons of each option based on your preferences—whether you want beaches or culture, adventure or relaxation, budget or luxury. It helps you make an informed decision without endless research.

Pro tip: Be specific about what matters to you: "We want warm weather, good food, and not too touristy" gives much better comparison points.

Learning & Understanding

Curiosity doesn't have an age limit. These prompts turn ChatGPT into your personal tutor for anything you want to learn or understand.

16"Explain [topic] like I'm hearing about it for the first time."

This is the ultimate beginner's prompt. It tells ChatGPT to assume zero prior knowledge and explain things from the ground up, using simple language and relatable examples. Perfect for understanding everything from cryptocurrency to climate change.

Pro tip: If the explanation is still too technical, ask "explain it even simpler" or "use an everyday analogy."
17"I want to learn [skill/hobby]. Create a beginner's plan for the first month."

Starting something new is exciting but overwhelming. This prompt breaks down the learning process into manageable steps so you don't feel lost. Whether it's painting, playing guitar, or learning Italian, you get a structured path forward.

Pro tip: Mention how much time you can dedicate ("30 minutes a day" or "a few hours on weekends") for a realistic schedule.
18"Summarise this in 3 key points: [paste long text]"

Long articles, documents, or emails can be exhausting to read. This prompt distills the essential information into three bullet points so you can quickly grasp the main ideas without reading every word. It's a huge time-saver.

Pro tip: Ask for "5 key points" if it's a longer document, or request "one sentence summary" for the absolute essentials.
19"What are the pros and cons of [decision]? I'm [age/situation]."

Big decisions—downsizing your home, retiring early, switching doctors—deserve careful thought. This prompt helps you think through both sides of a decision systematically, considering factors you might not have thought about on your own.

Pro tip: Include relevant details about your situation ("I live alone," "I have a fixed income") for more personalized pros and cons.
20"Quiz me on [topic] to test my understanding."

Learning something is one thing; retaining it is another. This prompt turns ChatGPT into a quiz master that tests your knowledge and helps reinforce what you've learned. It's especially useful if you're studying a new language, preparing for a test, or just want to check your comprehension.

Pro tip: Specify difficulty ("beginner level," "intermediate") or format ("multiple choice," "fill in the blank") for the type of quiz you want.

Home & Daily Life

From cooking dinner to understanding your electricity bill, these prompts make everyday tasks simpler.

21"I have [ingredients]. What can I make for dinner? Keep it simple."

Staring into the fridge wondering what to cook is a universal experience. This prompt solves that problem by suggesting recipes based on what you already have. The "keep it simple" instruction ensures you won't get elaborate multi-step recipes when you just want something quick.

Pro tip: Mention dietary preferences ("vegetarian," "low carb") or cooking equipment ("I have a slow cooker") for better suggestions.
22"Help me understand this [bill/contract/document] in plain English: [paste]"

Legal and technical documents are deliberately complicated. This prompt translates confusing terms, clauses, and fine print into language you can actually understand. It's empowering to know what you're signing or paying for.

Pro tip: Ask specific follow-up questions like "What does this clause mean?" or "Is this charge normal?" to dig deeper.
23"Suggest gift ideas for [person, age, interests]. Budget under [amount]."

Gift-giving shouldn't be stressful. This prompt generates thoughtful ideas based on the recipient's profile and your budget. It's especially helpful for people you don't know extremely well—like coworkers, neighbors, or distant relatives.

Pro tip: Mention any constraints like "nothing too personal," "practical gifts only," or "something they can use immediately."
24"My [appliance/device] is [problem]. Walk me through troubleshooting."

Before calling a repair person or buying a replacement, this prompt helps you diagnose the problem yourself. ChatGPT provides step-by-step troubleshooting instructions in plain language, which often solves the issue without any cost or hassle.

Pro tip: Be specific about the problem ("makes a loud noise when spinning," "won't turn on at all") and mention the brand/model if you know it.
25"Create a weekly cleaning/organization schedule for my [home type]."

Keeping a home clean is easier with a system. This prompt creates a realistic schedule that breaks tasks into daily, weekly, and monthly chunks so cleaning doesn't feel overwhelming. It's customized to your living situation—apartment, house, number of people, etc.

Pro tip: Mention physical limitations ("I can't lift heavy objects"), time constraints ("I work full-time"), or priorities ("I hate vacuuming but don't mind dishes") for a more practical plan.

Making Your Prompts Even Better

Now that you have 25 proven prompts, here are a few universal tips that will improve any prompt you write:

Be Specific

Vague prompts get vague answers. Instead of "help me with my garden," try "suggest beginner-friendly vegetables to grow in a small backyard garden in a temperate climate." The more details you provide, the more useful the response.

Give Context

Let ChatGPT know relevant background information. Your age, location, skill level, budget, or constraints help it tailor the answer to your actual situation rather than giving generic advice.

Ask for Simplification

If you get a response that's too technical or complicated, just say "explain that in simpler terms" or "pretend I'm a complete beginner." ChatGPT will adjust its language accordingly.

Don't Be Afraid to Iterate

Your first prompt doesn't have to be perfect. If the answer isn't quite right, refine your request: "make it shorter," "focus on the budget options," "remove the technical details." Think of it as a conversation, not a one-shot question.

Use Examples

If you want a specific style or format, show ChatGPT an example. "Write an email like this one, but about a different topic" works remarkably well.

Remember: ChatGPT is a tool, not a mind reader. The more thought you put into your prompts, the more useful the responses will be. But don't overthink it—you'll get better with practice.

Your Turn

These 25 prompts are just the beginning. The real power of ChatGPT comes from experimenting and discovering how it fits into your life. Maybe you'll use it to write letters to your grandchildren, plan meals for the week, or finally understand your investment statements.

Start with one or two prompts from this list. Try them out. Adjust them. See what works. Before long, you'll develop your own favorite prompts and find new ways to make ChatGPT genuinely useful.

And remember: there's no such thing as a "wrong" prompt. If it doesn't work the first time, just rephrase and try again. That's the beauty of having an endlessly patient assistant at your fingertips.
