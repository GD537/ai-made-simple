25 ChatGPT Prompts Everyone Should Know

By Sam Digital · February 2026

ChatGPT is only as useful as the questions you ask it. The difference between a vague, unhelpful answer and a brilliant, useful response often comes down to how you phrase your prompt.

Think of prompts as recipes: if you tell ChatGPT exactly what you need and give it the right ingredients, you'll get great results every time. But if you're too vague or leave out key details, you'll get something generic and disappointing.

In this guide, I've collected 25 of the most useful ChatGPT prompts for everyday life—organized by category so you can find exactly what you need. Each prompt includes an explanation of why it works and tips for customizing it to your situation.

Copy these prompts, tweak them to fit your needs, and watch ChatGPT become dramatically more helpful.

Writing & Communication

1. Write a Professional Email

"Write a professional email to [recipient] about [topic]. The tone should be [formal/friendly/apologetic]. Include [specific request or information]."

Why it works: Specifying the recipient, topic, tone, and goal gives ChatGPT everything it needs to craft a well-structured email.

Example: "Write a professional email to my landlord about a leaking faucet in my bathroom. The tone should be polite but firm. Ask for it to be fixed within a week."

2. Simplify Complex Text

"Explain this in simple terms a 12-year-old would understand: [paste complex text or concept]"

Why it works: Setting a specific reading level forces ChatGPT to remove jargon and use clear language.

Use for: Medical documents, legal terms, insurance policies, technical instructions.

3. Improve Your Writing

"Rewrite this to be more [clear/concise/friendly/formal]: [paste your text]"

Why it works: You specify the direction for improvement, so ChatGPT knows exactly what to fix.

4. Write a Thank You Note

"Write a warm thank you note to [person] for [what they did]. Mention [specific detail]. Keep it under 100 words."

Why it works: Including specific details makes the note feel personal, not generic.

5. Summarize an Article or Document

"Summarize this article in 3 bullet points, highlighting the main takeaways: [paste article text or URL]"

Why it works: Asking for bullet points and "main takeaways" ensures a concise, actionable summary.

Health & Wellness

6. Create a Simple Meal Plan

"Create a simple, healthy meal plan for [number of days] with easy-to-make recipes. I [don't like/am allergic to] [foods]. Budget: [amount]."

Why it works: Specifying dietary restrictions and budget ensures realistic, usable suggestions.

7. Get Exercise Suggestions

"Suggest 5 low-impact exercises I can do at home for [specific goal, e.g., back pain, flexibility, strength]. I have [equipment or limitations]."

Why it works: Mentioning your goal and limitations ensures safe, appropriate exercises.

Important: ChatGPT is NOT a doctor. Use these prompts for general information only. Always consult a healthcare professional for medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment.

8. Understand Medical Information

"Explain [medical term or condition] in simple, everyday language. What are the common symptoms and typical treatments?"

Why it works: Asking for "simple, everyday language" ensures you'll actually understand the explanation.

9. Track Habits with a Template

"Create a simple weekly habit tracker for [habits you want to track, e.g., drinking water, walking, reading]. Format it as a table I can print."

Why it works: Requesting a specific format (like a table) makes it immediately useful.

Travel & Planning

10. Plan a Trip Itinerary

"Create a [number]-day itinerary for [destination]. I enjoy [interests/activities]. Budget is [amount]. I prefer [travel pace: relaxed/packed]."

Why it works: Including your interests, budget, and preferred pace ensures recommendations match your style.

11. Get Packing List

"Create a packing list for a [trip length] trip to [destination] in [month/season]. I'll be [activities]. I want to pack [light/for any weather]."

Why it works: Season and activities determine what you need—this prompt captures both.

12. Find Hidden Gems

"Suggest 5 lesser-known, non-touristy places to visit in [destination] that locals recommend."

Why it works: Specifying "non-touristy" and "locals recommend" pushes ChatGPT toward authentic suggestions.

13. Budget Your Trip

"Estimate daily costs for a trip to [destination] including accommodation, food, transport, and activities. Budget level: [budget/mid-range/luxury]."

Why it works: Breaking down costs by category helps you plan realistically.

Learning & Personal Growth

14. Learn a New Skill Step-by-Step

"Teach me how to [skill] as a complete beginner. Break it down into simple steps with examples."

Why it works: "Complete beginner" sets the right level—no jargon, no assumptions.

Examples: "Teach me how to knit a scarf..." or "Teach me how to change a car tire..."

15. Get Book Recommendations

"Suggest 5 books for someone who enjoyed [book or author]. I prefer [genre or themes]."

Why it works: Referencing books you already liked helps ChatGPT match your taste.

16. Learn Basic Phrases in a Language

"Teach me 10 essential [language] phrases for [situation, e.g., dining out, asking directions]. Include pronunciation."

Why it works: Focusing on a specific situation makes the phrases immediately useful.

17. Explain Like I'm Five

"Explain [complex topic] as if I'm five years old."

Why it works: This classic prompt strips away jargon and uses simple analogies.

Example: "Explain how the stock market works as if I'm five years old."

Home & Practical Life

18. Troubleshoot a Problem

"My [appliance/device] is [problem]. What are the most common causes and how can I fix it myself?"

Why it works: Focusing on "most common causes" gives you practical starting points.

Example: "My washing machine is making a loud banging noise during the spin cycle..."

19. Organize Your Home

"Give me a step-by-step plan to organize my [room/space]. I have [time available] and [budget]. Priority: [goal, e.g., declutter, maximize storage]."

Why it works: Time, budget, and priority ensure realistic, actionable advice.

20. DIY Project Guidance

"Walk me through how to [DIY project] step-by-step. I'm a beginner. What tools and materials do I need?"

Why it works: "I'm a beginner" ensures instructions aren't too advanced.

21. Cleaning Schedule

"Create a weekly cleaning schedule for a [size] home with [number] people. Break it into daily, weekly, and monthly tasks."

Why it works: Breaking tasks by frequency makes it manageable and realistic.

Finance & Money

22. Create a Simple Budget

"Help me create a monthly budget. My income is [amount]. My fixed expenses are [list]. Suggest how to allocate the rest for savings, groceries, entertainment, etc."

Why it works: Providing your income and fixed costs lets ChatGPT tailor the budget to your reality.

23. Understand Financial Terms

"Explain [financial term or concept] in simple terms with an everyday example."

Why it works: Asking for an "everyday example" makes abstract concepts concrete.

Example: "Explain compound interest in simple terms with an everyday example."

24. Compare Options

"What are the pros and cons of [option A] vs. [option B] for [situation]? Which is better for someone who [your priorities]?"

Why it works: Including your priorities helps ChatGPT make a personalized recommendation.

Example: "What are the pros and cons of a 15-year mortgage vs. a 30-year mortgage for someone who wants to retire early?"

25. Negotiate a Better Deal

"Write a script I can use to negotiate [situation, e.g., a lower phone bill, a better price on a car]. Keep it polite but firm."

Why it works: A script gives you confidence and specific language to use.

How to Customize Any Prompt

These 25 prompts are starting points. Here's how to make them work even better for you:

Add Specific Details

The more context you provide, the better the answer. Include ages, budget, time constraints, preferences—anything relevant.

Specify the Format

Want a list? A table? A step-by-step guide? Say so: "Format this as a bullet list" or "Create a daily schedule in table format."

Set the Tone

Tell ChatGPT if you want formal, casual, funny, serious, etc. "Explain this in a friendly, conversational tone."

Request Examples

Add "Include 3 examples" or "Give me a sample" to make answers more concrete.

Set Constraints

"Keep it under 200 words" or "Limit to 5 suggestions" ensures concise, focused answers.

Ask for Alternatives

"Give me 3 different approaches" or "What are some variations?" ensures you have options.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Being Too Vague

Bad: "Plan a trip."
Good: "Plan a 5-day trip to Italy for a couple in their 60s who love food and history. Budget: $3,000."

Not Specifying the Audience or Context

Bad: "Explain blockchain."
Good: "Explain blockchain to someone who doesn't understand technology, using simple analogies."

Asking Multiple Questions at Once

Break complex requests into separate prompts. ChatGPT handles one clear question better than five jumbled together.

Forgetting to Review and Edit

ChatGPT's answers are starting points, not final drafts. Always review, personalize, and adjust to fit your needs.

Not Following Up

If the first answer isn't quite right, don't give up. Ask ChatGPT to revise: "Make this shorter" or "Can you include more detail about [topic]?"

Treating ChatGPT as Infallible

ChatGPT can make mistakes. Always verify important information—especially medical, legal, or financial advice.

Advanced Prompt Techniques

Once you're comfortable with basic prompts, try these advanced techniques:

Role Assignment

"Act as a [role] and [task]."

Example: "Act as a personal trainer and create a 4-week beginner workout plan for someone with knee pain."

Why it works: Giving ChatGPT a role focuses its expertise.

Chain of Thought

"Think through this step-by-step: [complex question]"

Why it works: Encouraging ChatGPT to "think" step-by-step improves reasoning and reduces errors.

Comparison Tables

"Create a comparison table of [options] based on [criteria]."

Example: "Create a comparison table of iPhone vs. Android based on ease of use, privacy, cost, and app selection."

Conversational Refinement

Don't treat ChatGPT like a search engine. Have a conversation:

  1. Ask your initial question
  2. Review the answer
  3. Ask follow-ups: "Can you expand on [point]?" or "Give me an example of that"
  4. Refine until it's exactly what you need

Building Your Own Prompt Library

As you use ChatGPT, you'll discover prompts that work perfectly for your needs. Save them!

How to Build Your Library

  • Keep a document (Google Doc, Notes app) with your favorite prompts
  • Organize by category (work, home, travel, etc.)
  • Note what worked and why
  • Refine prompts over time based on results

Share with Family

Once you've refined prompts that work great, share them with family members who might benefit. "Here's the prompt I use to plan trips—just fill in your details."

Practice Prompts to Try Right Now

Ready to test your skills? Try these practice prompts today:

  1. "Suggest a simple pasta recipe I can make in under 30 minutes with ingredients I likely already have."
  2. "Explain how mortgages work in simple terms, including what APR means."
  3. "Create a 3-day itinerary for visiting Seattle in summer, focused on nature and local food."
  4. "Write a polite email declining a social invitation because I'm not feeling well."
  5. "Teach me 5 essential Spanish phrases for ordering at a restaurant, with pronunciation."

See how the specificity in each prompt leads to useful, actionable answers? That's the power of well-crafted prompts.

The Bottom Line

ChatGPT is a powerful tool, but it's only as good as the questions you ask. The difference between mediocre and amazing results comes down to how you phrase your prompts.

Use these 25 prompts as your starting point. Customize them. Combine them. Experiment with different approaches. Over time, you'll develop an intuition for what works—and ChatGPT will become an indispensable part of your daily life.

Remember: there's no "wrong" way to use ChatGPT. The worst that happens is you get a mediocre answer and try again. So be curious, be specific, and don't be afraid to ask follow-up questions until you get exactly what you need.

The conversation is just beginning—and now you know exactly how to make it productive.
