Restore Old Family Photos with AI (Free Guide)

By Sam Digital · February 2026

Somewhere in your home, you probably have old family photos tucked away in albums or shoeboxes. Faded images of your parents when they were young. Your grandparents' wedding day. Your own childhood, captured in sepia tones or washed-out colors.

These photos are precious—they're windows into the past, connections to people you love. But time hasn't been kind to them. They're scratched, faded, torn, or so dark you can barely make out the faces.

Here's the incredible news: AI can bring these photos back to life. You can restore color to black-and-white images, sharpen blurry faces, repair tears, and enhance faded photos—all from your phone or computer, often for free.

In this guide, I'll walk you through exactly how to restore your old family photos using AI tools. No technical skills required—just a desire to preserve your family's history.

What AI Photo Restoration Can Do

Before we dive into the tools, let's understand what's possible:

  • Colorize black-and-white photos. AI can add realistic color to old black-and-white or sepia-toned images.
  • Enhance quality. Sharpen blurry faces, improve contrast, and make dark photos brighter.
  • Repair damage. Remove scratches, tears, stains, and creases.
  • Restore faded photos. Bring back lost detail and vibrancy to photos that have deteriorated over time.
  • Remove backgrounds. Isolate people from cluttered or damaged backgrounds.

The results can be stunning—photos you thought were beyond saving can look nearly brand new.

Important: AI does its best to guess what colors and details should be, but it's not perfect. Restored photos are interpretations, not exact recreations. But they're often close enough to feel magical.

Tool #1: MyHeritage Photo Tools (Best for Beginners)

MyHeritage is a genealogy website, but they offer some of the best free AI photo restoration tools available. You don't need a paid subscription to use the basic features.

What MyHeritage Offers

  • Photo Enhancer: Sharpens faces and improves overall quality
  • Photo Colorizer: Adds color to black-and-white photos
  • Photo Repair: Fixes scratches, tears, and other damage
  • Animate Photos: Makes still photos "come alive" with subtle movement (optional—can be fun or creepy, depending on your perspective!)

Step-by-Step: Using MyHeritage

  1. Go to MyHeritage.com and create a free account using your email.
  2. Click "Photo Tools" in the top menu (or go directly to myheritage.com/photo-tools).
  3. Choose a tool: Start with "Enhance" if your photo is blurry or dark, or "Colorize" if it's black-and-white.
  4. Upload your photo. You can upload from your computer, phone, or even take a photo of a physical picture.
  5. Wait a few seconds. The AI processes the image automatically.
  6. Review the result. You'll see a side-by-side comparison of the original and enhanced version.
  7. Download the restored photo. Save it to your device.

MyHeritage Limitations

  • Free accounts can restore a limited number of photos per month (usually 5–10).
  • The free version adds a small watermark to some photos.
  • For unlimited access, you'd need a paid subscription (around $10–15/month).

Best For

Beginners who want fast, easy results with minimal effort. Great for enhancing and colorizing photos.

Tool #2: Google Photos (Built-In AI Features)

If you use Google Photos to store your pictures (and you probably do if you have an Android phone), you already have access to some powerful AI photo editing tools.

What Google Photos Can Do

  • Auto-enhance: One-tap improvement to brightness, contrast, and color
  • Portrait Light: Adjusts lighting on faces to make them clearer
  • Sharpen details: Makes blurry photos clearer
  • Remove unwanted objects: Erase background clutter or imperfections

Step-by-Step: Using Google Photos

  1. Open the Google Photos app on your phone or go to photos.google.com on your computer.
  2. Upload or select the old photo you want to restore.
  3. Tap "Edit" at the bottom of the screen.
  4. Tap "Suggestions" at the top—Google's AI will automatically suggest improvements. Tap one to apply it.
  5. Manually adjust: Use sliders for brightness, contrast, saturation, and sharpness to fine-tune the photo.
  6. Try "Portrait Light" (if available) to brighten faces in dark photos.
  7. Save a copy. Google Photos saves the original, so you can always revert.

Google Photos Limitations

  • Doesn't colorize black-and-white photos automatically (you'd need a separate tool for that).
  • Works best for relatively recent photos; very old, damaged photos might need more specialized tools.
  • Some advanced features require a Google One subscription (usually $2–10/month).

Best For

Quick touch-ups and enhancements of faded or dark photos you already have stored in Google Photos.

Tool #3: Remini (Mobile App for Photo Enhancement)

Remini is a popular mobile app specifically designed for photo enhancement. It's excellent at sharpening faces in old, blurry photos.

What Remini Does

  • Dramatically sharpens blurry faces
  • Enhances overall photo quality
  • Works well on very old or low-resolution photos

Step-by-Step: Using Remini

  1. Download Remini from the App Store (iPhone) or Google Play Store (Android).
  2. Open the app and tap "Enhance."
  3. Select a photo from your phone or take a picture of a physical photo.
  4. Wait for processing—usually takes 5–30 seconds.
  5. View the result. Remini will show you a before-and-after comparison.
  6. Save the enhanced photo to your phone.

Remini Limitations

  • Free version allows a limited number of enhancements per day (usually 3–5).
  • Ads can be intrusive.
  • Full version costs around $5–10/month.

Best For

Sharpening blurry faces in old photos. The results can be almost miraculous for very low-quality images.

Tool #4: Colorize.cc (Free Online Colorization)

If you specifically want to colorize black-and-white photos and don't want to sign up for anything, Colorize.cc is a simple, free option.

Step-by-Step: Using Colorize.cc

  1. Go to colorize.cc in your web browser.
  2. Drag and drop your black-and-white photo onto the page, or click to upload.
  3. Wait a few seconds while the AI colorizes the image.
  4. Download the colorized version.

Best For

Quick, no-frills colorization when you don't want to create an account or download an app.

Step-by-Step Restoration Workflow

Here's a recommended workflow to get the best results when restoring an old photo:

Step 1: Scan or Photograph the Original

If you have a physical photo, you need a digital version first.

  • Use a scanner if you have one (set it to at least 300 DPI for good quality).
  • Take a photo with your phone in bright, even lighting. Avoid shadows and glare. Use the Google Photos "Scan" feature (look for it in the app's menu) for best results.

Step 2: Enhance Quality First

Start by improving the overall quality before colorizing:

  • Use Remini or MyHeritage Enhancer to sharpen faces and improve clarity.
  • Adjust brightness and contrast using Google Photos if the image is too dark or washed out.

Step 3: Repair Damage

If the photo has scratches, tears, or stains:

  • Use MyHeritage Photo Repair to automatically fix major damage.
  • For minor imperfections, use the "retouch" or "healing" tool in Google Photos to manually remove them.

Step 4: Colorize (If Desired)

Once the quality is good, add color:

  • Use MyHeritage Colorizer or Colorize.cc to add color to black-and-white photos.

Step 5: Save and Share

  • Save multiple versions: Keep the original, the enhanced version, and the colorized version.
  • Back up to cloud storage: Upload to Google Photos, iCloud, or Dropbox so you never lose them.
  • Print or share: Order prints to display, or share with family via email or messaging apps.

Real-World Example: Restoring Grandma's Wedding Photo

Let me walk you through a complete restoration process.

The Photo

A black-and-white photo of my grandmother's wedding from 1952. The photo is faded, has a crease down the middle, and the faces are blurry.

Step 1: Scan the Photo

I used the Google Photos "Scan" feature on my phone, holding the photo flat on a table with good lighting.

Step 2: Enhance Quality

I uploaded it to MyHeritage Photo Enhancer. Within seconds, the faces became much clearer and sharper.

Step 3: Repair the Crease

I used MyHeritage Photo Repair to remove the crease. It did about 80% of the work automatically. For the remaining visible line, I used Google Photos' "retouch" tool.

Step 4: Colorize

I ran it through MyHeritage Colorizer. The AI added realistic skin tones, colored the wedding dress ivory, and made the flowers in the bouquet pink and white.

The Result

A photo I could barely make out now looks vibrant and clear. I printed it, framed it, and gave it to my mother for her birthday. She cried. It was the best $10 frame I've ever bought.

Tips for Best Results

Start with the Best Quality Original

The better the scan or photo of your original, the better the AI restoration. Use good lighting, avoid glare, and scan at high resolution.

Use Multiple Tools

Don't rely on just one tool. Enhance with Remini, repair with MyHeritage, colorize with Colorize.cc—combine tools for the best outcome.

Don't Over-Edit

It's tempting to crank every slider to maximum, but subtlety is key. Small adjustments often look more natural than dramatic changes.

Keep the Original

Always save the original photo (both the physical and digital version). Restorations are interpretations—the original is irreplaceable.

Batch Process Similar Photos

If you have many photos from the same era, process them in batches. You'll develop a feel for what settings work best for that type of photo.

Be Patient with AI

Sometimes the first result isn't perfect. Try the photo in different tools or adjust your settings. It might take a few attempts to get it right.

Organize all restored photos in a dedicated folder on Google Drive or Dropbox. Share access with family so everyone has these memories preserved.

Use Google Photos or iMovie to create a slideshow of restored photos, set to music. Perfect for family gatherings or milestone birthdays.

Use in Genealogy Projects

If you're researching your family tree, restored photos add visual richness to your family history.

Preserving Physical Photos for the Future

While you're digitizing and restoring photos, take steps to preserve the originals:

  • Store in a cool, dry place—avoid attics, basements, or anywhere with moisture.
  • Use acid-free albums and storage boxes to prevent further deterioration.
  • Keep out of direct sunlight, which fades photos over time.
  • Handle with clean hands or wear cotton gloves to avoid transferring oils.
  • Don't use tape or glue on photos—it causes permanent damage.

When to Hire a Professional

For most photos, AI tools do an excellent job. But for truly precious or severely damaged photos, consider hiring a professional photo restoration service.

When to Consider a Pro

  • The photo is one-of-a-kind and irreplaceable
  • Damage is extensive (torn into pieces, heavily water-damaged)
  • You want museum-quality restoration
  • The photo has complex coloring or details AI struggles with

Where to Find Professionals

  • Local photo labs often offer restoration services
  • Online services like MemoryCherish.com or Faded.com
  • Freelance platforms like Fiverr or Upwork (check reviews carefully)

Expect to pay $30–$200+ depending on the complexity and quality you want.
