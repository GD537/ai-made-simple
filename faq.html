Frequently Asked Questions

Honest answers to the most common questions about AI.

Q Is ChatGPT free?

Yes. The basic version of ChatGPT is completely free. You can create an account at chat.openai.com and use it without paying anything.

There IS a paid version called ChatGPT Plus ($20/month) that's faster and uses a more advanced AI model. But for most people, the free version is excellent — and I recommend using it for at least 3 months before considering an upgrade.

Q Is AI safe to use?

Generally yes. Using ChatGPT or Google Gemini is as safe as using Google Search or sending email.

Just follow three simple rules:

1. Don't share sensitive information (passwords, bank details, personal ID numbers)
2. Don't trust AI blindly — verify important facts
3. Don't use AI to make medical, legal, or financial decisions without consulting a professional

For a full guide, read: Is AI Safe? What You Actually Need to Know

Q I'm not tech-savvy. Can I still use AI?

Absolutely. If you can send a text message, you can use AI. It's literally just typing a question into a box and reading the response.

You don't need to understand how it works. You don't need special skills. Adults over 55 are the fastest-growing group of AI users — because once people try it, they discover it's genuinely helpful.

✅ Writing emails and letters
✅ Planning trips and activities
✅ Understanding medical terms
✅ Meal planning and recipes
✅ Learning new topics
✅ Explaining confusing documents

Q Is ChatGPT always right?

No. This is crucial to understand. AI sometimes makes confident-sounding mistakes. This is called "hallucination."

AI is great for drafts, ideas, and explanations — but always verify important facts from a trusted source. Think of it like a very smart friend who occasionally gets their facts mixed up.

Q What's the difference between ChatGPT and Google?

They're different tools for different jobs:

Use Google when you want to FIND something:
• A specific website
• A business phone number
• Today's news
• Opening hours

Use AI when you want to UNDERSTAND or CREATE something:
• Explain a concept
• Write a draft
• Brainstorm ideas
• Plan something

Full comparison: AI vs Google: When to Use Which

Q Will AI take my job or my children's jobs?

AI is changing jobs more than replacing them — similar to how computers, email, and smartphones changed work without eliminating it.

The people most at risk aren't those who might be replaced by AI — they're those who refuse to learn how to use AI. The technology itself is a tool. Those who learn to use it will thrive.

Q Do I need to download anything?

No. You can use ChatGPT and Google Gemini directly in your web browser — no download required.

There ARE apps available for phones if you prefer, but they're optional. Just go to chat.openai.com or gemini.google.com in any browser.

Q Does AI remember what I tell it?

Within one conversation: Yes. It remembers everything you've said in that session.

Between conversations: It depends on your settings. ChatGPT has an optional "Memory" feature that can remember facts about you across sessions. You can turn this off in Settings → Personalisation → Memory.

Either way, still don't share sensitive information like passwords or bank details.

Q How do I get better answers from AI?

Be specific. The more detail you give, the better the response.

❌ "Write me an email"

✅ "Write a polite but firm email to my electricity company about being overcharged $47 on my February bill. I've been a customer for 12 years and want a refund."

Include relevant context: your age, situation, preferences, limitations. AI can't read your mind — tell it what it needs to know.
